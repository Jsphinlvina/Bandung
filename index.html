Welcome to Bandung

Bandung is the capital city of Indonesia province of West Java. It has population of 2,444,160 within its city limts, making it the fourth most populous city in Indonesia. Greater Bandung (Bandung Basin Metropolitan Area/BBMA) is the country's third-largest metropolitan area, with over eight million inhabitants

Culinary

1. Seblak

Seblak is a typical Sundanese dish that is known to come from the Parahyangan region with a savory and spicy taste. Made from wet crackers with fine spices consisting of garlic with kencur. However, what distinguishes seblak from similar foods is that seblak generally uses orange crackers with cikur or Sundanese spices and is served with spicy sauce.

2. Mie Kocok

Mie Kocok is a noodle dish with a beef broth flavor typical of the city of Bandung. Mie Kocok is the same as Bakso, but Mie Kocok has more variety of toppings. Meatballs only consist of yellow noodles, vermicelli, tetelan. Meanwhile, Mi Kocok consists of yellow noodles served in thick beef broth, sliced kikil, bean sprouts, meatballs, lime, and sprinkled with sliced celery, spring onions, and fried onions. Mie Kocok has a more savory and concentrated taste than Bakso and that is the distinguishing and characteristic feature of Mie Kocok.

3.Karedok

Karedok is one of the typical Sundanese food in Indonesia. Karedok is made with raw vegetable ingredients, including; cucumber, bean sprouts, cabbage, long beans, sweet potatoes, basil leaves, and eggplant or leunca. While the sauce is peanut sauce made from red chili, garlic, kencur, peanuts, tamarind water, sugar kawung, salt, and shrimp paste. One of the characteristics of karedok is using oncom bakar.
